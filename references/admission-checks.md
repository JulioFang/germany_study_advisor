# Admission Checks

## Source Hierarchy

Use current official sources whenever possible:

1. Target program admissions page and admission regulations.
2. University central admissions page.
3. uni-assist program profile or VPD rules when applicable.
4. anabin/KMK recognition information for credential equivalence.
5. APS requirements for applicants whose education history triggers APS.
6. DAAD as a broad discovery source, not the final authority for program-specific rules.

## Bachelor / Studienkolleg

Check:

- Whether the school-leaving credential gives direct subject-specific access, direct general access, or only Studienkolleg access.
- Whether the applicant needs TestAS, APS, Feststellungsprüfung, language certificates, entrance exam, portfolio, or aptitude test.
- Whether the program is NC/restricted admission and whether grades are ranked after formal eligibility.
- Whether the program language is German, English, or mixed.

For Chinese applicants, pay special attention to gaokao pathway, completed university semesters, institution recognition, and APS certificate rules. Verify current rules from APS, anabin/KMK, uni-assist, or the university.

## Master

Check:

- Recognized Bachelor degree or equivalent.
- Required field match: exact major, related major, or listed eligible degrees.
- ECTS or credit prerequisites by subject area, such as math, CS, business, economics, engineering, lab work, thesis, or statistics.
- Minimum grade or grade band, if any.
- Language requirements and whether scores can be submitted after application.
- Required documents: module handbook, course descriptions, grading scale, CV, motivation letter, recommendation letters, thesis abstract, GRE/GMAT/TestAS, APS.
- Selection procedure: formal threshold, points system, interview, essay, ranking, or lottery after eligibility.

When subject credits are ambiguous, map transcript modules to the program's requirement categories in a small table: requirement, user's evidence, gap, confidence.

## Email Escalation

Suggest emailing admissions when:

- The applicant's degree or institution status is unclear.
- The program requires subject-specific credits but course mapping is borderline.
- The deadline page conflicts with the application portal.
- The user relies on late language-score submission.

Draft emails in concise English or German and include applicant facts, program name, intake, and the exact question.
