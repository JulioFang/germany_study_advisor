# Costs and Fees

## Always Verify Current Amounts

Tuition, semester contributions, blocked-account requirements, health insurance prices, housing costs, and state-level non-EU fees change. For concrete amounts, browse official sources and give the date of the source.

Preferred sources:

- University fee pages for semester contribution, tuition, and administrative fees.
- State ministry or university pages for non-EU tuition policies.
- DAAD and German missions for visa and blocked-account context.
- Studentenwerk or student services pages for housing and living costs.
- Public health insurance providers for student insurance ranges.

## Fee Categories

Separate:

- **Tuition**: program or state tuition, often zero at many public universities but not universal.
- **Semester contribution**: student services, administration, student body, and sometimes public transport.
- **Private university fees**: can be substantial and program-specific.
- **Special program fees**: MBA, executive, continuing education, non-consecutive master's, distance learning, or international-track fees.
- **Application fees**: uni-assist handling fee, document certification, translations, test reports, courier.
- **Visa/living proof**: blocked account or other accepted funding proof.

## Cautions

- Do not say "Germany is tuition-free" without qualifiers.
- Baden-Wuerttemberg, Bavaria program fees, private universities, second-degree rules, non-consecutive programs, and special master's programs can change the answer.
- Munich, Frankfurt, Hamburg, Stuttgart, Berlin, and smaller university towns can have very different rent pressure.
