# Program Matching Rubric

## Inputs

Collect:

- Target degree and field.
- Must-have constraints: language, public/private, budget, location, intake, deadline, work-study needs.
- Academic profile: degree, major, GPA, transcript modules, thesis, research, internships, publications.
- Language and tests: German, English, GRE/GMAT/TestAS.
- Preferences: applied/theory, career target, university type, city size, ranking importance.

## Scoring

Use an interpretable 100-point rubric when ranking multiple programs:

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Formal eligibility | 30 | Degree recognition, subject match, ECTS, minimum grade |
| Academic competitiveness | 20 | GPA/rank, relevant modules, thesis/research |
| Language and tests | 15 | Meets thresholds, timing risk |
| Career/research fit | 15 | Curriculum, labs, industry, thesis options |
| Cost and logistics | 10 | Tuition, city cost, housing, visa timing |
| Application feasibility | 10 | Deadlines, documents, APS/uni-assist/VPD |

Label fit:

- 85-100: strong target.
- 70-84: realistic target with manageable gaps.
- 55-69: reach or conditional target.
- Below 55: low fit unless the user changes constraints.

## Shortlist Shape

Build balanced lists:

- 2-3 safer targets where formal requirements are clearly met.
- 3-5 realistic targets.
- 1-3 reach targets if the user accepts uncertainty.

For each program, include why it fits, why it may fail, what to verify, and the next deadline.

## Matching Caveats

- A high fit score is not an admission guarantee.
- Ranking-heavy NC programs and small cohorts can be unpredictable.
- Strong work experience may help applied programs but rarely replaces formal ECTS requirements unless rules say so.
- Do not recommend private programs as "safe" without explaining cost and recognition.
